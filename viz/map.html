Three properties, pick two

A curve made of steps can have three nice properties: it can be a smooth thread (dimension 1, not a crinkly fractal), it can be triple-free (no three points ever in a line), and it can be built from a finite menu of steps (bounded hops, as Erdős #193 demands). You can always get two of the three — but the corner where all three meet appears to be empty.

How to read it. Each corner is a property you'd want. A curve that achieves two of them lives on the edge between those corners — and is pushed away from the third. The moment curve gives up the finite menu; the straight line gives up triple-freeness; our walk gives up exact dimension 1, settling for a barely-crinkled fractal thread (dimension 1.10) — the one vertex a finite menu actually allows.

Why this is the whole problem. Erdős #193 fixes two corners — it requires triple-free and a finite menu — and doesn't even ask for dimension 1. It only asks whether such a curve can exist at all: whether the dimension can stay bounded (a thread) instead of swelling toward the space-filling blob. Our construction is the claim that it can, and that it lands at dimension 1.10. The one lemma still standing between us and a full proof — Lemma R — is exactly the statement that our object really does sit on that bottom edge: a genuine crinkly thread, never secretly drifting up toward the blob.
