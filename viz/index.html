Can a walk on the 3D grid avoid every three-in-a-line, forever?

Erdős Problem #193 asks whether an infinite walk with steps from a finite set can visit lattice points with no three ever collinear. Open since 1979. This project attacks it with exact, AI-assisted computation under human direction. It builds record-breaking walks by repeating one move — enlarge, twist, stitch — at ever-larger scales, then tests whether a finite safety controller could always choose the next stitch without creating a forbidden line. Every geometric decision is integer arithmetic that anyone can re-run.

Current status: the record is verified; an infinite walk is not proved.

The earlier claim that bounded local crowding would guarantee a legal stitch was wrong: a candidate can be killed by a line through two points arbitrarily far away. The old height-two action region is now ruled out exactly: it has 601 words overall, only eight at the repeated step-20 corridor, and no legal entry in its correlated 8-by-8 second-stitch matrix. A much larger lattice-envelope filter retains 8,367,038 strict zero-envelope words and 10,252,458 ordered-envelope words. Those counts prove acyclicity only for contiguous, direction-blind candidate chains — not for silent lines, newly born secants, unrelated cursor imports, or the complete global poison set. A strict zero-envelope construction using globally fresh yz fibres has completed all 2,457 L5 stitches and stores 8,268 points; a separate exact audit has now verified every first-survivor choice and the full natural-order chain. This is one finite certificate, not an all-level selector. A direction-only ban on the two known latent spectra fails already at inherited anchors 30/33, whose secant has J=11/3. Restricting to short connectors does not close the induction: the exact greatest fixed point of all length-2 and length-3 connector words is empty, so every recurrent closed policy must use a length-4 or length-5 word. Mixed-length growth exactly 3 remains open. The exact ghost family is spaced like 9n, which gives a cutoff at each finite level but no uniform cutoff as the walk grows. Exact birth equations are known; weighted local-lemma and shell-transfer arguments remain proposed routes, not proofs.

Two equations that carry the project

$$\text{collinear}(x,y,z)\;\Longleftrightarrow\;(y-x)\times(z-x)=\mathbf{0}$$

The rule, exactly. Three grid points line up precisely when this cross product of integer vectors is the zero vector — so every check in the project is exact integer arithmetic, never a floating-point approximation. The record walk passes this test for all 5 quadrillion of its triples.

$$K(s,P)=\{w\in D_s:\ w\text{ creates a forbidden incidence with }P\},\qquad D_s\setminus K(s,P)\ne\varnothing$$

The actual proof target. At each gap of type $s$, the finite domain $D_s$ contains all allowed connector words. The poison set $K(s,P)$ contains every word killed by a collision or by a secant through any two points already placed, including points far from the gap. A successful proof needs a finite, reachable safety state that always leaves at least one word outside this exact poison set and whose chosen word leads to another safe state. The state must preserve the realized path order and recent connector choices, while a separate ranked or contracting tail controls distant endpoints and secants. Why a local view is not enough

Connector legality is global. A new connector point is illegal if it lies on a line through any two earlier points. Those endpoints need not be close to the pending gap, so a bound on local crowding — even a very strong one — cannot by itself prove that some connector survives. Exact replay confirms the issue quantitatively, and a general counterexample can keep every small ball sparse while remote pairs seal every first move. The potential repair is to carry nearby geometry exactly and summarize distant endpoint/secant effects by birth level or 3-adic shell, with a proved rank or contraction that prevents an unbounded tail from accumulating. The current exact trace confirms that joins with other points must be retained. The x-parallel closure also proves that a currently empty mask is not enough to discard a line and that adding the current exact mask and action still leaves short recurrences. The Bellman audit proves outward escape only after a line is outside the core; all observed recurrence sources remain inside. At L9, old tagged endpoints still poison connector words through joins with anchors born at several earlier levels. A sound tail state must preserve those hidden future effects and correlations. Exact ghost spacing gives a deterministic cutoff for any one bounded finite level, but that cutoff grows with the walk and is not an all-level tail theorem. The exact endpoint-and-line birth operator is now written down. A complete generic transfer census shows why simple averaging does not close it: the all-descendant role kernel has spectral radius at least two, although the finite continuously effectful lattice-T contact kernel is nilpotent. Silent ghosts, births, legality conditioning, and cursor imports are precisely what remain. A weighted local-lemma proof or a mask-valued shell transfer built from the exact operator remains a research route until a uniform reachable-birth bound and a positive survivor inequality are certified.

Origin story

This project got unstuck because of a dishwasher. Every early attempt built self-copying patterns that stacked like plates — each scale aligned with the last, and alignment is exactly what this problem punishes (we eventually proved all such aligned constructions must fail — the Perron–Frobenius no-go in the research log). Then his collaborator Amanie Ismail shared a Reddit post about loading bowls in a dishwasher, and Erik noticed the bowls do something plates don’t: they nest at a tilt — the same shape repeated, each one offset and rotated from the last. Translated into mathematics, that became the construction: enlarge the walk by a matrix that also twists it, then reconnect the enlarged anchors with short legal paths. That idea — imbrication — is the backbone of the record walks. The later proof audit showed that twisting and sparse local geometry are not sufficient on their own; the remaining work is the global connector-safety controller described above.

The project is built and steered by Erik Kalviainen (ekalvi), a software engineer in Canada, on an always-on Mac in his homelab, with Amanie Ismail collaborating on ideas and direction. The mathematics, code and computation use a multi-model AI research workflow, including Anthropic and OpenAI systems, under Erik’s direction. Claims are separated into proofs, exact finite computations, sampled evidence and conjectures; the exact programs and data are published so the important results can be rerun independently.

By the numbers. More than a week of work since 11 July 2026, 120+ commits, 190+ code files, and 8 construction rounds to the 311,738-point record — plus many adversarial audits that retired attractive but invalid proof claims. Idea credit where it’s due: to Amanie, and to whoever loaded that dishwasher.
